Diary for Dion and Duane

My kind of town, Chicago is

2007-09-19 to 2007-09-24

Touchdown at Chicago`s O`Hare airport and we aree given another public transport system to negotiate.  Fortunately, due to track repair works that put Englands to shame, crossing Chicago takes a good 3 hours.

 

Eventually we find ourselves in the Loyolla universtiy part of the city.  Tierd and unable to drink in our hostel we fall into a local bar.  Being surrounded by drunk students all pretending to be 21 was fun for a while.  Our waitress was lovely and had her friends rustle up the names of a couple of bars in town we might enjoy and some American lads gave us each a free bud-light (something to do with a promotion, but beggars can`t be choosers)

 

With money running out fast and the girls safely home it was time to revert to budget living - fortunately Chicago is a lovley city just to wander round.  Massive buildings wherever the eye wanders and friendly locals who will be more then happy to point you in the right direction.  One necessary expense was to indulge in one of Chicagos famous deep pan pizzas and it was lovely although i`m suer my life expectancy has been reduced dramatically.

 

At night we discovered a free method of getting drunk.  Along with our new freinds Mike and Guy we wandered student dorms and gatecrashed the first party we came across.  The people were a little wary at first, but the British charm came out and the girls made us feel welcome (the guy`s kept there distance all night - it was a little awkward, vut at least there was no trouble.)

 

With Guy and Mike bing the sort of people who research places, we discovered we could go to the top of the Hankok building and only have to buy a drink (much cheaper than going to the observation deck and a floor higher) and when we got up there the service was so slow we were able to sneak out again.  No better price than free.

 

Havin managed to get ourselves invited to a costume party the next night we turned up ready for a crazy time, but it turns out all the students are Jesuits and so don`t really drink.  That didn`t seem like a reason for us not to however, and so it was a fun night and only a couple of people asked if i knew the Queen.

 

Chicago, i can`t remember what i expected but it was a lot better i`m sure.  Great people, awesome food and beautiful parks along the lakes.  What a skyline!!  Let`s hope they get the olympics in 2016!
