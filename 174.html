Diary for Dion and Duane

Sweet dreams my LAX

2007-08-30 to 2007-09-03

Change of country, time zone and date.  Que the longest day ever which caused Thursday to run for 42 hours.  There seemed only one way to cope with such a body shattering experience and that was to invoke international jet-lag laws and have a drink (well before the 4 o`clock watershed!)  With ourselves in a very American British pub we staring putting away the Guinness and then wandered along Santa Monica pier being terribly British and probably a little annoying.

With the required hangover settled in we planned our stay in LA.  A trip down to Venice beach to see the street performers, Muscle beach (Duane and I decided not to work out) and some of the most honest beggars in the world - they only wanted money for weed!

When in LA you have to take on Hollywood and so we borded the bus (wonderful system, i don`t know what people complain about) and found the avenue of the stars.  Tourist central and full of lookalikes (a poor attempet at Alistor Moody attempted to cast a spell on me - lets hope it didn`t work).  Feeling like finding some stars we bought a map of there houses and hit Beverly Hills.  Foudn the playboy mansion and wandered down Rodeo, but then decided enough was enough.  We briefly visited the Tar pits where Mamoths had famously been found after they fell in and then went home.

Duane was determind to do something cultered in LA and so we went to the Getty.  Lots of Americans crowded round something by Manet (wasn`t that good) and we saw lots of art.  the building was the most impressive thing about the gallery.  Spectacular architecture, with the promise of views of LA if the smog ever lifts.

LA the place of the beautiful people, cars and excessive heat.  Time for a touch more culture as we head to `Frisco`
