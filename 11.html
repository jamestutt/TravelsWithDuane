Dion and Duane
7th Sep 2007 - 19th Sep 2007
Road Trip: we've been having fun, driving in the sun.

In the words of Gemma and Alison:  "So with 'Hotel California' blasting out of the stereo we took to the road to travel the famous Highway 1 coastal route bound for San Diego.  With stops in Santa Cruz, Cambria, and even 'The OC' itself - Newport Beach - we had an amazing few days and a great start to our 'extended adventure.' [unable to say goodbye the girls had to extend their trip by two weeks]. There was a slight hiccup in Santa Barbara when ion kept navigating us into a car park that 'wasn't there'.

Back on the road, after the slight delay we moved onwards to San Diego with spectacular scenery all the way.  The Californian coast is stunning - rivalling New Zealand in its rugged beauty and surpassing it in that it wasn't so damn cold when we stopped to take photographs!

We stopped in San Diego for a couple of nights which gave us all the chance to indulge in some fantastic spanish-inspired  cuisine  (paella and sangria - yum!) [this was where my Margarita binge began] and more importantly gave Alison the opportunity for her long-awaited visit to San Diego's famous Zoo.  Thank god for Dion accompanying her and allowing Duane and I to chill out in the animal-free park instead! [Amen to that, cheers mate].

An epic drive across the border to Arizona was next on the trip and after 8 hours of travelling (with only 3 CD's we were already tiring of to choose between!) [and the girls skipping all the good tracks] we finally arrived in Flagstaff, gateway to the Grand Canyon.  Our day at the Canyon was absolutely incredible.  None of us really had any idea what to expect of this immense natural wonder but it's safe to say that we were all pretty much blown away on first sight.  We spent the day in the national park picnicking with the squirrels whilst looking over the Canyon (shame about the view hey Duane?!) and then headed back for a chilled out evening.

The next day was spent in 'Dion's world' with a visit to the Meteor Crater in the middle of the desert.   At first neither Alison or myself thought we would be particulary impressed by a big hole in the ground (even if it was created by something from outer space!) but having our personal physics-buff with us meant that the whole visit was so much more interesting. [No one heard Dion complain about having to spend a day looking at fluffy animals in a zoo]

After a quick stop at the Hoover Dam we crossed into our third state and travelled across the cacti-covered Nevada desert heading for Vegas Baby! We hit Las Vegas Boulevard with music blaring, hanging out of the windows with our cameras ready to snap as many pics as possible of the casinos, hotels and general neon-lit craziness of 'the Strip' 

Our fantastic hostel - complete with pool and hot-tub! - was the scene of a drunken first evening's frolics and a first day's recovery before we pulled ourselves together, dressed ourselves up and hit the Venetian hotel and casino for a fabulous dinner (thank god for credit cards!)  An undoubted highlight of the evening was the 45 minutes we spent watching the fountains outside of the Bellagio (there's a show every 15 minutes and we stayed for 3 of them - Viva Las Vegas!) [not to mention the yard long Margaritas]

So having gone out with a bang in Vegas, our last night of what was an amazing road trip was spent in Yosemite national park, relaxing with a delicious dinner and sleeping in log cabins before rising early for our drive back to San Francisco and our last night of our travels."

Cheers girls (and I don't just mean for writting my blog for me) it was an excellent adventure in the desert. Gemma your watch is still ticking and I've not worn it in the shower yet. See you soon. xxx
